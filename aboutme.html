Background

I am a Principal ML Engineer & Researcher with over a decade of experience building AI systems at scale. Currently leading a team of 20+ engineers at Atlassian on Generative Personalization and Foundation Models — driving LLM/SLM pre-training, post-training (fine-tuning, preference alignment), and large-scale personalization.

Previously, I founded and led the Personalization ML team at Attentive (0→1, 15+ engineers), built reply/home feed ranking systems at Twitter that served tens of millions of QPS, and worked on international shipping ML at Amazon. My engineering work has driven >30% engagement gains at Twitter, 10x training speedups, and 20% latency improvements in production.

I hold an MS in Computer Science from UC San Diego, where I studied under Prof. Julian McAuley in the Machine Learning and Recommender Systems lab.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of NLP, recommender systems, and deep learning. I have published in top-tier venues including ACL (#1 in Computational Linguistics), WSDM (15% acceptance rate), and RecSys, accumulating 1,150+ citations. OpenAlex ranks my research impact in the top 1% globally.

Researchers from 30+ countries across 6 continents have cited my work. My Kaggle datasets (News Category, Sarcasm Detection, Clothing Fit) have been downloaded 77,000+ times and adopted for coursework at institutions including the University of Paris-Saclay.

I serve as a reviewer and program committee member at 20+ leading AI conferences including SIGIR, CIKM, RecSys, ICML, KDD, and TheWebConf.

Engineering at Scale

I specialize in designing low-latency, large-scale ML systems for production. My systems have served 10M+ QPS and processed hundreds of millions of data points. At Twitter, a patent was filed based on my reply ranking work.

I am the co-author of Sculpting Data for ML (used in Deeplearning.ai's NLP course and Google's TF certification), and create educational content on Udacity and LinkedIn Learning.
